Mobile phone registration is a vital regulatory requirement that ensures all cellular devices sold in the Philippines are safe for public use and compatible with national network infrastructures. This process focuses on technical compliance, radio frequency safety, and the prevention of counterfeit or substandard devices.

### Health and Safety (SAR)
The NTC strictly enforces **Specific Absorption Rate (SAR)** limits to protect users from excessive radio frequency exposure. All mobile phone models must undergo testing in accredited laboratories to demonstrate that they fall within the safe exposure levels defined by international standards.

### Network Compatibility
To ensure seamless connectivity, the NTC verifies that mobile devices support the frequency bands authorized for use by Philippine telecommunications providers. This prevents technical issues such as dropped calls, poor data speeds, or interference with other wireless services.

### Importer Responsibility
Importers are legally required to obtain NTC Type Acceptance for every model they bring into the country. Each unit sold to the public must bear the official NTC sticker as proof of regulatory compliance.
